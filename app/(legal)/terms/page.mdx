## What a credit is

- **1 credit is 1 question.** A credit is deducted when you send a question to the
  assistant.
- **The assistant's own tool steps are free.** If answering your question takes fifteen
  tool calls, the question still costs one credit.
- **You are charged for asking, not for answering.** The credit is spent when the
  question is sent. If you stop the assistant part way through its reply, the credit is
  still spent.
- If the assistant fails because our provider was unreachable or returned an error
  before replying, the credit is automatically returned to you.

Everyone gets **up to 10 free questions per calendar month**, which reset at the start
of each month. Free questions are used before any credits you have bought.

The free allowance is a ceiling, not a guarantee: to stop one person creating unlimited
free questions, free questions are also limited per network address, pooled across
devices. Several people sharing one internet connection may get fewer free questions
each than they would alone. Buying credits removes this entirely.

## Restoring your balance

Your credits are held against a random identifier your browser generates. They are also
anchored to the email address you bought with, so you can move them.

To move a balance to a new computer or a fresh install: open the workbench chat, choose
**Restore purchases**, enter your purchase email, and we email you a six-digit code. The
code expires after ten minutes, allows five attempts, and you can request at most three
codes an hour.

Restoring **moves** your balance. It does not copy it: the old install's balance is
merged into the new one.

If you clear your browser profile and no longer have access to your purchase email, we
cannot identify your balance.
